# Outreach Drafting Guidelines

Draft only. This skill never sends anything -- these openers are for the
user to review, edit, and send themselves through their own email/LinkedIn/
whatever channel they choose.

## Structure of a good opener

1. **Reference the specific public thing they said or did** -- not a
   paraphrase so generic it could apply to anyone. If the evidence was a
   forum post about a workaround breaking down, mention that workaround by
   name, not just "I saw you're dealing with some challenges."
2. **State the connection plainly** -- why you're reaching out, in one
   sentence, without hype.
3. **One clear, low-friction ask** -- a short call, a quick reply, feedback
   on a specific thing. Not "let's hop on a call to discuss synergies."
4. **Keep it short.** Three to five sentences. Nobody responds well to a
   cold message that reads like a pitch deck.

## Tone by mode

- **design-partners** -- casual, feedback-seeking, explicitly low-commitment
  ("would love five minutes of your reaction to this, no pitch")
- **b2b** -- professional, references the specific business trigger (a
  hire, an expansion, a migration), leads with relevance not features
- **standard/quick/deep/community** -- respectful and specific by default;
  match the register of the community the signal came from (a Hacker News
  commenter and a G2 reviewer read differently)

## Hard boundaries

- **No manufactured urgency.** Don't invent scarcity or pressure that isn't there.
- **No personal information beyond what's professionally public.** Reference
  their public post, their company, their public role -- not personal details
  found elsewhere that happen to be technically discoverable.
- **No pretending to be a peer or a fellow user if you're not.** Be
  straightforward about who's reaching out and why.
- **Respect stated opt-outs.** If someone's public profile or post says
  "not interested in vendor outreach" or similar, exclude them from the
  outreach draft entirely -- note their evidence in research if relevant, but
  don't draft them an opener.
- **Never send it.** Drafting is in scope. Sending an email, DM, connection
  request, or any other message is a separate action the user takes
  themselves, deliberately, after reviewing the draft.
